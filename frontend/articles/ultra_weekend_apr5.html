Ultra Weekend Recap — April 5–6

By Ultramarathon Connect • 04/07/2025

The first weekend of April brought spring energy to the ultramarathon scene with fast finishes, scenic routes, and serious mileage across the country. From North Carolina’s trails to the Idaho high desert, here’s how the weekend unfolded:

Umstead 100 – Raleigh, North Carolina

Date: April 6, 2025
Distance: 100 miles
Course: 12.5-mile loop in Umstead State Park (mostly gravel road)
Weather: Sunny and mild, 64°F

This East Coast classic delivered again with a sold-out field and supportive community vibes. Top finishers:

  • 1st Male: Eric Cho – 15:38:27
  • 1st Female: Kate Thomas – 17:29:04

Official Website | Full Results

Pickled Feet 24/12/6 Hour Runs – Eagle, Idaho

Date: April 5, 2025
Distances: Timed event (6h/12h/24h)
Course: 2.5-mile dirt loop at Eagle Island State Park
Weather: Breezy, 58°F

Runners pushed through all-day and all-night loops, clocking big miles under the stars:

  • 24-Hour Male Winner: James Monroe – 125 miles
  • 24-Hour Female Winner: Sarah Greene – 113 miles

Official Website

Crown King Scramble 50k – Phoenix to Crown King, Arizona

Date: April 5, 2025
Distance: 50k
Course: Steep jeep roads, 5,700 feet of climb
Weather: Hot at lower elevations, cool at the finish (40s–80s)

Runners climbed from desert floor to pine forest finish—one of Arizona’s most iconic point-to-point ultras:

  • 1st Male: Paul Dillard – 4:34:19
  • 1st Female: Brooke Atkinson – 5:07:46
