Ultra Weekend Recap — February 1–2

By Ultramarathon Connect • 02/03/2025

The first weekend of February 2025 saw ultrarunners braving diverse terrains and challenging conditions across the globe. Here's a recap of the notable events and results:

Lake Sonoma 100 – Healdsburg, California

Date: February 1–2, 2025
Distance: 100M
Official Website

The Lake Sonoma 100 challenged runners with its significant elevation gain and 90% singletrack terrain. The course offered a unique experience, combining technical trails with scenic views.

Jed Smith Ultra Classic – Sacramento, California

Date: February 1, 2025
Distances: 50K, 50M
Official Website

Celebrating its 47th edition, the Jed Smith Ultra Classic provided runners with a fast, flat course along the American River Parkway. The event attracted both seasoned ultrarunners and newcomers.

Febapple Frozen Fifty – Maplewood, New Jersey

Date: February 1, 2025
Distances: 50K, 50M
Full Results

Runners faced icy trails and cold temperatures in this challenging event. Zephyr Larson led the 50-mile race with a time of 8:32:00, followed by John Blasingame and Kyle DelGuidice.

TARCtic Frozen Yeti 30-Hour Ultra – Weston, Massachusetts

Date: February 1–2, 2025
Format: 30-Hour Timed Event
Full Results

Participants braved frigid temperatures and challenging conditions in this endurance event. Matthew Rogers topped the field, covering 115 miles in 29:06:00.

Arrowhead 135 – International Falls, Minnesota

Date: February 1–2, 2025
Distance: 135M
Official Website

Known for its extreme cold, the Arrowhead 135 tested runners' resilience on a 135-mile course through northern Minnesota. Participants faced temperatures as low as -45°F.

Yukon Arctic Ultra – Whitehorse, Yukon, Canada

Date: February 1–2, 2025
Distances: Marathon, 100M, 300M, 430M
Official Website

Billed as the toughest ultramarathon in the world, the Yukon Arctic Ultra challenged participants with sub-zero temperatures and remote wilderness. Runners navigated the course on foot, skis, or bike.

Congratulations to all the runners who participated in these events! Your dedication and perseverance continue to inspire the ultrarunning community.
